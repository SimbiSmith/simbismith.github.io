What are production queues?

A production queue is a system for managing the flow of jobs or tasks through a production process. It is a way to prioritize and schedule work so that it is completed efficiently and effectively.

Production queues can be used in a variety of industries, including manufacturing, construction, and service industries. For example, a manufacturing company might use a production queue to manage the flow of products through its assembly line. A construction company might use a production queue to manage the schedule of its workers and equipment. And a service company might use a production queue to manage the flow of customers through its checkout line.

Here at Simbi Smith, we have tried and tested procedure for creating our production queues

We create custom production queues to produce high volumes of specific products, both in-house as well as for external factories.
The process of creating a production queue can be broken down into the following steps:

  • Identify the jobs or tasks that need to be included in the queue. This may involve gathering information from different departments or teams within your organization.
  • Prioritize the jobs or tasks. This can be done based on a variety of factors, such as importance, urgency, and due date.
  • Select a queue type. The two most common queue types are first-in-first-out (FIFO) queues and priority queues. FIFO queues process jobs in the order in which they are received, while priority queues process jobs in order of importance.
  • Implement the queue. This may involve creating a whiteboard or spreadsheet to track the queue, or using a computer system to manage the queue.
  • Monitor the queue and make adjustments as needed. It is important to monitor the queue on an ongoing basis to ensure that jobs are being processed efficiently and effectively.

Creating a production queue can be a complex task, but it is a worthwhile investment. A well-managed production queue can help to improve the efficiency and productivity of your business.
